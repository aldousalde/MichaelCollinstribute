Michael Collins

Former NASA astronaut who flew on the Gemini 10 and Apollo 11 missions

Timeline of Michael Collin's life

  • 1930 - Born in Rome, Italy in October 31, 1930
  • 1952 - graduated from the U.S. Military Academy at West Point
  • 1959 - served as a test pilot until 1963 at Edwards Air Force Base in California and logged more than 4,200 hours of flying time
  • 1963 - selected as a member of the third group of NASA astronauts
  • 1966 - served as pilot (his first flight) of Gemini 10, a three-day mission
  • 1969 - remained in lunar orbit while fellow crewmembers Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin descended to the moon's surface in the lunar module during the Apollo 11 mission and became one of the first men to step onto the surface of another planetary body
  • 1970 - retired from the Air Force as a major general, left NASA and became assistant secretary of state for public affairs
  • 1971 - joined the Smithsonian Institution as director of the National Air and Space Museum
  • 1974 - wrote "Carrying the Fire"
  • 1976 - wrote "Flying to the Moon and Other Strange Places"
  • 1980 - became vice president of LTV Aerospace and Defense Co.
  • 1985 - left LTV Aerospace and Defense Co. and started his own company as an independent consultant writing and lecturing about space
  • 1988 - wrote "Liftoff: The Story of America's Adventure in Space"
  • 1990 - wrote "Mission to Mars"
  • 2021 - passed away at the age of 90
“[A] true pioneer and lifelong advocate for exploration... he helped our nation achieve a defining milestone...

Michael remained a tireless promoter of space...

His own signature accomplishments, his writings about his experiences, and his leadership of the National Air and Space Museum helped gain wide exposure for the work of all the men and women who have helped our nation push itself to greatness in aviation and space. There is no doubt he inspired a new generation of scientists, engineers, test pilots, and astronauts.

...Whether his work was behind the scenes or on full view, his legacy will always be as one of the leaders who took America's first steps into the cosmos. And his spirit will go with us as we venture toward farther horizons.”

--NASA Administrator Steve Jurczyk

Collins was awarded honorary degrees form six universities. Decorations and awards he received included the Presidential Medal for Freedom, the Robert J. Collier Trophy, the Robert H. Goddard Memorial Trophy and the Harmon International Trophy.
